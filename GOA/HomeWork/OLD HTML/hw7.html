Georgian history

history is a long narrative marked by ancient kingdoms like Colchis and Iberia, a medieval "Golden Age" under the Bagrationi dynasty, and a persistent struggle against invasions from various empires. After a period of decline and annexation by the Russian Empire in the 19th century, Georgia experienced a brief period of independence before becoming part of the Soviet Union. It finally regained full independence in 1991 after the collapse of the Soviet Union.

Early ancient history

  1. Ancient kingdoms, such as Diaokhi (c. 12th century B.C.) and Colchis (c. 600 B.C.), existed along the Black Sea.
  2. The ancient kingdom of Iberia, founded around 302 B.C., was centered in modern-day eastern Georgia
  3. In the early 4th century, Iberia officially adopted Christianity, a pivotal moment that helped unify the Georgian people
