About Assembly

Kathleen Booth is credited with inventing the first assembly language, and she also designed the first assembler and autocode for the computer systems at Birkbeck College, University of London. The term "assembler" was later popularized by Maurice Wilkes, David Wheeler, and Stanley Gill in 1951


Assembly is a low-level programming language that acts as a human-readable representation of a computer's machine code. It uses mnemonic codes for instructions and allows programmers to communicate directly with the hardware, providing more control and optimization for specific tasks


Assembly language is still relevant and in active use today in highly specialized fields, although it is not used for general-purpose application development. Its scope is smaller than in past decades, but it remains indispensable for specific low-level programming tasks.
